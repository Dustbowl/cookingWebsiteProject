Cheeseburger Cups
1 pound ground beef 1/2 cup ketchup 2 tablespoons brown sugar 1 tablespoon prepared mustard 1-1/2 teaspoons Worcestershire sauce 1 tube (12 ounces) refrigerated buttermilk biscuits 1/2 cup cubed Velveeta
10
Cook beef over medium heat until no longer pink; drain fluid. Stir in the ketchup, brown sugar, mustard and Worcestershire sauce. Remove from the heat; set aside. Press each biscuit onto the bottom and up the sides of a greased muffin tray. Spoon beef mixture into cups; top with cheese cubes. Bake at 400° for 14-16 minutes or until golden brown. 

Lemon Parsley Pasta
8 oz. thin spaghetti 1 fresh lemon 2 cloves garlic, minced 1/2 cup chopped fresh parsley 1/8 tsp freshly cracked black pepper 1 Tbsp olive oil 2 Tbsp butter 2 Tbsp grated Parmesan 1/4 tsp salt
3
Boil large pot of water, add the pasta until tender (about 7-8 minutes). Drain the pasta in a colander. While the pasta is cooking, zest the lemon 1 Tbsp. Chop parsley and combine it with the Parmesan, lemon zest, and pepper in a bowl. While the pasta is draining, add the olive oil and minced garlic to the pot used to cook the pasta. Sauté the garlic in the oil over medium heat for 1-2 minutes, or just until it becomes fragrant. Remove pot from the heat and add the drained pasta and butter to the pot with the garlic. Toss the pasta until the residual heat has melted the butter and the pasta is coated in garlic and butter. Add the parsley Parmesan mixture to the pot and toss to coat again. Squeeze about 1 Tbsp of the lemon juice over the pasta and season with salt. 

Garlic Parmesan Kale Pasta
1 bunch 1/2 lb kale 1/2 lb angel hair pasta 2 Tbsp olive oil 2 Tbsp butter 2 cloves garlic, minced 1/4 cup grated Parmesan Pinch salt and pepper 
5
Pull kale leaves from stems and tear them into small 1-2 inch pieces. Rinse the kale well in a colander under cool, running water and allow it to drain. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Break the pasta in half, add it to the boiling water, and cook about 7 minutes. Drain the pasta in a colander. While the pasta is cooking, add the olive oil, butter, and minced garlic to another large pot or skillet. Cook over medium heat for 1-2 minutes, or until the garlic is soft and fragrant. Add the kale and continue to sauté until the kale has wilted and has turned a deep green color or about 5-7 minutes. Turn the heat off. Add the drained pasta to the pot with the sautéed kale. Toss the pasta and kale together. Allow them to cool. You want the pasta warm, but not hot enough to melt the Parmesan. Season the pasta and kale with salt and pepper. Add the grated Parmesan cheese and toss to coat.

Garlic Parmesan Roasted Cauliflower
1 head califlower, 1/4 cup grated parmesan, 1/2 tsp garlic powder, 1/4 tsp salt, 1/4 tsp pepper, 2 tbsp olive oil, 1 tbsp chopped parsley (optional)
3
Preheat oven to 400F Rinse cauliflower well, cut into bite size pieces. Combine parmesan, garlic powder, salt, and pepper in small bowl. Place cauliflower pieces in a bowl and drizzle olive oil on top then the parmesan mixture, then toss until covered. Spread cauliflower over baking sheet, transfer into preheated oven. Roast cauliflower for 30 minutes stirring halfway through.

Teriyaki Chicken Thighs
3 pounds boneless skinless chicken thighs 3/4 cup sugar 3/4 cup reduced-sodium soy sauce 1/3 cup cider vinegar 1 garlic clove, minced 3/4 teaspoon ground ginger 1/4 teaspoon pepper 4 teaspoons cornstarch 4 teaspoons cold water Hot cooked rice, optional 
10
Place chicken in a slow cooker. In a small bowl, mix sugar, soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, ginger and pepper and pour over chicken. Cook, covered, on low 4-5 hours or until chicken is tender. Move chicken to a serving platter; keep warm. Transfer cooking juices to a small saucepan; and skim. Bring cooking juices to a boil. In a small bowl, mix cornstarch and cold water until smooth and stir into cooking juices. Return to a boil; cook and stir 1-2 minutes or until thickened. Serve with chicken and, if desired, rice.

Slow Cooker Mac and Cheese
1 package (16 ounces) elbow macaroni 1/2 cup butter, melted 4 cups shredded cheddar cheese, divided 1 can (12 ounces) evaporated milk 1 can (10-3/4 ounces) condensed cheddar cheese soup, undiluted 1 cup 2% milk 2 large eggs, beaten 1/8 teaspoon paprika
10
Cook macaroni according to package directions and drain. Place in a slow cooker and add butter. In a large bowl, mix 3 cups cheese, evaporated milk, condensed soup, 2% milk and eggs. Pour over macaroni then stir to combine. Cook, covered, on low for 3-1/2 to 4 hours or until a thermometer reads at least 160°. Sprinkle with remaining cheese. Cook, covered, on low until cheese is melted, 15-20 minutes longer. Sprinkle with paprika.

Cajun Sausage and Rice Skillet
14 oz. Andouille sausage 1 Tbsp cooking oil 1 bell pepper 1 tsp smoked paprika 1/2 tsp dried oregano 1/2 tsp dried thyme 1/4 tsp garlic powder 1/4 tsp onion powder 1/8 tsp cayenne pepper 1/8 tsp freshly cracked black pepper 1 15oz. can fire roasted diced tomatoes 1 cup long grain white rice 1.5 cups chicken broth 2 green onions, sliced 
9
Slice the sausage into ¼-½ inch thick slices. Add the sausage and cooking oil to a deep skillet or Dutch oven and sauté over medium heat until the sausage is well browned. While the sausage is cooking, dice the bell pepper. Once the sausage is browned, add the bell pepper to the skillet and continue to sauté for about one more minute. Add the smoked paprika, oregano, thyme, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne, and black pepper to the skillet with the sausage and bell pepper and continue to sauté for one minute more to toast the spices. Add the fire roasted diced tomatoes (with juices), rice, and chicken broth to the skillet. Stir to combine. Place a lid on the skillet, turn the heat up to medium-high, and allow broth to come to a full boil. Once it reaches a boil, turn the heat down to low and let the skillet simmer for 20 minutes. Remove the skillet from the heat and let it rest, with the lid on, for an additional 5 minutes. Remove the lid, fold the sausage and rice to redistribute. Top with green onions.

Baked Chicken Nuggets
1 cup breadcrumbs, 1/2 tsp garlic powder, 1/2 tsp smoked paprika, 1/4 tsp salt, pepper, 2 Tbsp neutral oil, 1 chicken breast, 1 egg
4
Preheat Oven to 425 and place cooling rack. In a bowl combine breadcrumbs, garlic powder, smoked paprika, and pepper. Stir until combined and drizzle oil over then stir. Cut chicken breast into small pieces. Dip pieces into cracked egg, then roll in breadcrumbs until fully coated. Transfer pieces onto drying rack and bake 23-25 minutes.

Garlic Parmesan Kale Pasta

5
Pull kale leaves from stems and tear them into small 1-2 inch pieces. Rinse the kale well in a colander under cool, running water and allow it to drain. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Break the pasta in half, add it to the boiling water, and cook about 7 minutes. Drain the pasta in a colander. While the pasta is cooking, add the olive oil, butter, and minced garlic to another large pot or skillet. Cook over medium heat for 1-2 minutes, or until the garlic is soft and fragrant. Add the kale and continue to sauté until the kale has wilted and has turned a deep green color or about 5-7 minutes. Turn the heat off. Add the drained pasta to the pot with the sautéed kale. Toss the pasta and kale together. Allow them to cool. You want the pasta warm, but not hot enough to melt the Parmesan. Season the pasta and kale with salt and pepper. Add the grated Parmesan cheese and toss to coat.

Cajun Sausage and Rice Skillet
14 oz. Andouille sausage 1 Tbsp cooking oil 1 bell pepper 1 tsp smoked paprika 1/2 tsp dried oregano 1/2 tsp dried thyme 1/4 tsp garlic powder 1/4 tsp onion powder 1/8 tsp cayenne pepper 1/8 tsp freshly cracked black pepper 1 15oz. can fire roasted diced tomatoes 1 cup long grain white rice 1.5 cups chicken broth 2 green onions, sliced 
9
Slice the sausage into ¼-½ inch thick slices. Add the sausage and cooking oil to a deep skillet or Dutch oven and sauté over medium heat until the sausage is well browned. While the sausage is cooking, dice the bell pepper. Once the sausage is browned, add the bell pepper to the skillet and continue to sauté for about one more minute. Add the smoked paprika, oregano, thyme, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne, and black pepper to the skillet with the sausage and bell pepper and continue to sauté for one minute more to toast the spices. Add the fire roasted diced tomatoes (with juices), rice, and chicken broth to the skillet. Stir to combine. Place a lid on the skillet, turn the heat up to medium-high, and allow broth to come to a full boil. Once it reaches a boil, turn the heat down to low and let the skillet simmer for 20 minutes. Remove the skillet from the heat and let it rest, with the lid on, for an additional 5 minutes. Remove the lid, fold the sausage and rice to redistribute. Top with green onions.

Baked Chicken Nuggets
1 cup breadcrumbs, 1/2 tsp garlic powder, 1/2 tsp smoked paprika, 1/4 tsp salt, pepper, 2 Tbsp neutral oil, 1 chicken breast, 1 egg
4
Preheat Oven to 425 and place cooling rack. In a bowl combine breadcrumbs, garlic powder, smoked paprika, and pepper. Stir until combined and drizzle oil over then stir. Cut chicken breast into small pieces. Dip pieces into cracked egg, then roll in breadcrumbs until fully coated. Transfer pieces onto drying rack and bake 23-25 minutes.

Lemon Parsley Pasta
8 oz. thin spaghetti 1 fresh lemon 2 cloves garlic, minced 1/2 cup chopped fresh parsley 1/8 tsp freshly cracked black pepper 1 Tbsp olive oil 2 Tbsp butter 2 Tbsp grated Parmesan 1/4 tsp salt
3
Boil large pot of water, add the pasta until tender (about 7-8 minutes). Drain the pasta in a colander. While the pasta is cooking, zest the lemon 1 Tbsp. Chop parsley and combine it with the Parmesan, lemon zest, and pepper in a bowl. While the pasta is draining, add the olive oil and minced garlic to the pot used to cook the pasta. Sauté the garlic in the oil over medium heat for 1-2 minutes, or just until it becomes fragrant. Remove pot from the heat and add the drained pasta and butter to the pot with the garlic. Toss the pasta until the residual heat has melted the butter and the pasta is coated in garlic and butter. Add the parsley Parmesan mixture to the pot and toss to coat again. Squeeze about 1 Tbsp of the lemon juice over the pasta and season with salt. 

Garlic Parmesan Kale Pasta
1 bunch 1/2 lb kale 1/2 lb angel hair pasta 2 Tbsp olive oil 2 Tbsp butter 2 cloves garlic, minced 1/4 cup grated Parmesan Pinch salt and pepper 
5
Pull kale leaves from stems and tear them into small 1-2 inch pieces. Rinse the kale well in a colander under cool, running water and allow it to drain. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Break the pasta in half, add it to the boiling water, and cook about 7 minutes. Drain the pasta in a colander. While the pasta is cooking, add the olive oil, butter, and minced garlic to another large pot or skillet. Cook over medium heat for 1-2 minutes, or until the garlic is soft and fragrant. Add the kale and continue to sauté until the kale has wilted and has turned a deep green color or about 5-7 minutes. Turn the heat off. Add the drained pasta to the pot with the sautéed kale. Toss the pasta and kale together. Allow them to cool. You want the pasta warm, but not hot enough to melt the Parmesan. Season the pasta and kale with salt and pepper. Add the grated Parmesan cheese and toss to coat.
